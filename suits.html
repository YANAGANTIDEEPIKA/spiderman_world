Spider-Man Suits

Classic Suit

Artist Steve Ditko designed it in 1962, and it's a look that has changed very little over the years.

Fun fact: It wasn't until 1984, in issue #8 of Secret Wars, that Spider-Man traded in his classic red and blue for a new suit.

First appearance: Amazing Fantasy #15 (1962)

Symbiotic suit

The Symbiotic Suit, also known as the Black Suit, was Peter Parker's alternate costume. He obtained it thanks to an alien machine he found on Battleworld that saved him from wearing his usual battle-torn attire.

First appearance: Marvel Super Heroes Secret Wars Vol 1 8

Enhanced Suit

The MCU broke with Spider-Man movie tradition by having Tony Stark design Spider-Man's regular suit. While based on the classic suit, this suit has several added black elements and gadgets that help Peter in his job as a Neighbor and Friend in Queens.

First appearance: Spider-Man Far From Home

Stealth Suit

The Stealth Suit was one of the first technologies created by Peter Parker at the head of Horizon Labs. This suit was specifically designed to combat the threat of the new Hobgoblin and his sonic screech. Among its particularities, in addition to being able to become invisible, it stands out for being invulnerable to sonic attacks.

First appearance: Amazing Spider-Man Vol 1 650 (February 2011)

Iron Spider Suitr

This Tony Stark-designed suit is much more durable than Spidey's regular suit, and features several retractable arms similar to those of Doctor Octopus. The suit (designed by concept artist Ryan Meinerding) also has a distinct color scheme, reflecting Tony Stark's love of red and gold.

First appearance: Amazing Spider-Man Vol 1 529

Spider-armor: MK II suit

The Spider-Sense is one of Peter Parker's most useful abilities, and the only reason he's still alive. So when he completely loses it, Peter had to design a new suit for his diminished reflexes. This suit was designed by artist Marcos Martin.

First appearance: Amazing Spider-Man #656 (2011)

Spider-armor: MK III suit

This incarnation of the Spider-Man armor (designed by artist Stefano Caselli) came late in Peter's career at Horizon Labs. His most advanced creation to date, it was designed to counter the abilities of each member of the Sinister Six. It was a key tool in the storyline "Earth's Ends," when Doctor Octopus grew tired of leading the Six to end the world through global warming.

First appearance: Amazing Spider-Man #682 (2012)

Spider-armor: MK IV suit

For a brief period of time, Peter Parker was at the top of his game when he ran his own company, Parker Industries, and became one of the most powerful tycoons in the world. This allowed him to create a new suit (designed by artist Alex Ross) made of liquid nanotechnology. With an advanced HUD and access to drones and other high-tech gadgets, he could reach further than ever before.

First appearance: Amazing Spider-Man Vol. 4 #1 (2015)

Secret War Suit

In Secret War, Nick Fury recruited a group of heroes to travel to Latveria and try to overthrow its ruler, Lucia Von Bardas. Clandestine missions like that require matching attire, so Spider-Man is given a new stealth suit (designed by artist Gabriellle Dell'Otto) with only a few hints of his traditional costume.

First appearance: Secret War #1 (2004)

Negative suit

Normally, the Negative Zone falls under the jurisdiction of the Fantastic Four, but Spider-Man ended up traveling to that dimension to save a group of New Yorkers stranded there. In the process, his suit was transformed from the usual red and blue to a warped black and white look (one designed by artist John Romita, Jr.).

First appearance: Spider-Man #90 (1998)

Future Foundation Suit

The Future Foundation is a philanthropic foundation founded by Reed Richards to improve the future of humanity. Spidey came to the foundation as Johnny Storm's (the Human Torch) final wish before he ""died""

Electrical insulation suit

Electro is a dangerous suit, one that has come closer than a few years to killing Spider-Man over the years. This suit (designed by artist Steve Skroce) was Peter's first of many attempts to counter Max Dillon's electrical powers. The padding on the suit gave Peter an advantage, but he eventually had to improve the original design to keep up with the years.

First appearance: Amazing Spider-Man #425 (1997)

Dark suit

"Dark" is one way of referring to this suit, which debuted during a 'dark' time in Spidey's career. In Spider-Man/Deadpool, Wade Wilson was hired to kill Peter Parker, not realizing that Peter was also his fellow Spider-Man. Deadpool had to retrieve Peter's soul from the afterlife and from the clutches of Mephisto, paving the way for a more sinister suit, designed by Ed McGuinness.

First appearance: Spider-Man/Deadpool #8 (2016)

Speed suit

This cool armor (designed by artist Adi Granov) is another of the suits designed specifically for Insomniac's game. As the name suggests, this suit increases Spidey's speed and allows him to use his inertia as a weapon.

First appearance: Marvel's Spider-Man (2018)

Incarnate scary suit

Many of Spider-Man's alternate suits give him new abilities, but only one gives him the power of a god. Artist Stuart Immonen designed this new suit, which is made of the same metal as Thor's hammer. His primary weapon is a pair of bladed gauntlets. Unfortunately, Spidey only got to use his new suit once before Odin destroyed it.

First appearance: Fear Itself #7 (2011)

Cyber Suit

Before the confrontation with the Sinister Six, Spider-Man was injured in one arm. To help him and allow him to fight, Deathlok gave him a cybernetic suit with various enhanced powers.

First appearance: Amazing Spider-Man #619

Cosmic Suit

The Enigma Force chose the wall-crawler to possess its wonderful power. With this suit, Spider-Man had the ability to fly, shoot cosmic rays and modify matter.

First appearance: Amazing Spider-Man #198 (1992)

Advanced Suit

Made with unstable molecules to increase all of Spider-Man's abilities, such as his strength, speed and spider-sense, in addition to possessing great spy technology.

First appearance: Amazing Spider-Man 61 (2021)

Lasts MK I

The first MK I version, courtesy of Terry Kavanagh and Alex Saviuk, was designed to provide Spider-Man with additional protection against a group of (forgettable) villains calling themselves the New Enforcers. Made from a pseudo-metallic compound developed by Peter Parker at Empire State University, the suit robbed the wall-crawler of speed and agility, but was bulletproof. During the battle, the suit was destroyed by acid.

First appearance: Web of Spider-man 100 (1985)

Last Rattle Suit

The Spider-Man of Earth-312500 is much older and more pessimistic than the traditional Peter Parker. This is reflected in his suit (designed by John Romita Jr.), which is more 'everyday' than the traditional Spider-Man design.

First appearance: Amazing Spider-Man Vol. 2 #58 (2003)

Cosmic Suit

In many versions of the Spider-Man mythology, Peter Parker had a short career as a professional wrestler before being brought back to reality by his murder by Uncle Ben. His wrestling suit is constructed from random clothing items, hinting at the superhero costume Peter will eventually own. The in-game wrestling suit is modeled after the one designed by artist Mark Bagley in Ultimate Spider-Man.

First appearance: Ultimate Spider-Man #3 (2000)

Spider-Man 2099 continues

In the year 2099, a new hero named Miguel O'Hara emerged to fill the void left by Peter Parker. This suit was designed by artist Rick Leonardi, and many fans say that it is one of the best aspects of Spider-Man. Thanks to the futuristic technology of this time, Miguel's suit has a ton of added benefits, including being constructed of indestructible "unstable molecules" and a 'shell' of air that allows him to glide.

First Appearance: Amazing Spider-Man #365 (1992)

Spider-Man 2099 Suit (White Version)

Despite the popularity of the Spider-Man 2099 suit, Marvel wanted to give Miguel O'Hara a new look (courtesy of artist Kris Anka) for his third series. This new suit came about when Miguel was stuck in the present-day Marvel Universe. Luckily for him, Peter Parker (who was then in charge of a multinational corporation called Parker Industries) gave him a new suit with the most advanced technology of 2015.

First appearance: Spider-Man 2099 Vol. 3 #1 (2015)

Scarlet suit

The Scarlet Spider is Ben Reilly, a clone of Peter Parker created by the Jackal. Ben's return kicked off the infamous clone storyline, with Peter and Ben trying to figure out which one of them was the original Spider-Man. This suit (designed by artist Tom Lyle) reflects Ben's status as a loner and underprivileged.

First appearance: Web of Spider-Man #118 (1994)

Scarlet Suit (Kaine)

With Ben Reilly gone, a cured and miraculously reformed Kaine took up the mantle of the Scarlet Spider and set out on his own adventures. He would debut in his new suit. A design, it must be said, tremendously elegant and stylish.

First appearance: Scarlet Spider vol. 2 (2012)

Top Suit

After adopting the name Superior Spider-Man, Otto first changed the blue of the original costume to black. The spider on his back became thicker and the eyes on the mask were replaced with lenses with different functions. The fingers on his hands gained retractable claws, as did the feet, which were divided like a bird's leg.

First appearance: Amazing Spider-Man vol. 1 #700 (2013)

Lasts Superior Otto

Little by little, Otto modified the suit even more, adding more technological advances: paralyzing claws, the ability to communicate with his robotic inventions and "spider arms" that came out of the back, in a small nod to his former identity of Doctor Octopus.

First appearance: Amazing Spider-Man vol. 2 #700 (2013)

Spider-Ghost Suit

The Earth-11638 version of Spider-Man is a Peter Parker who didn't learn about power and responsibility until much later. It was only after his death that he got a second chance at true herodom, becoming a vengeful spirit and rising from the grave. His character design (by artist Lee Garbett) combines the classic Spider-Man look with a dose of Ghost Rider.

First appearance: Amazing Spider-Man Annual #38 (2011)

Spider-Punk lasts

On Earth-138, Hobie Brown takes over as Spider-Man from Peter Parker. His punk-rock-influenced take on the Spider-Man suit (designed by artist Olivier Coipel) reflects the overall tone of this universe. Hobie is a revolutionary who leads the people of New York in a revolution against the oppressive regime of President Osborn.

First appearance: Amazing Spider-Man Vol. 3 #10 (2014)

Black Suit

Peter designed his own costume from his Uncle Ben's World War I uniform. It consists of aviator goggles, a helmet to protect his identity, a bulletproof vest with a spider printed on the back, and a fedora hat, accompanied by a long black coat.

PFirst appearance: Spider-Man Noir Vol 1 (2008)

Lasts MK I

Because his body was reinforced with technology in most of his body, he gained impressive superhuman strength, surpassing the strength he had before becoming a Cyborg, gaining more durability against physical attacks, and his robotic arm is equipped with a laser cannon, and on his back he has a sonic amplifier, with certain self-repair capabilities, and being able to connect with external technological devices.

First appearance: Superior Spiderman 33 (2014)

Lasts 1602

Yes, although it's incredible but true, Spiderman also travels to the past! The 1602 series takes place in England and mixes Marvel characters with historical elements and events.

First appearance: Spider-Man 1602 Vol 1 (2009)

Miles Morales Suit

After officially becoming Spider-Man, SHIELD gave Miles the official red and black suit to replace the homemade costume the teenager had made for himself. Miles' suit, designed by Sara Pichelli, has become extremely popular.

First appearance: Ultimate Comics Spider-Man vol. 2 #5 (2012)
