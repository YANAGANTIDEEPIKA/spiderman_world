Spider-Verse Figures

Spider-Man

During a student visit to the Faculty of Exact and Natural Sciences at the UBA, sponsored by the General Techtronics Corporation, Peter was bitten by a spider that had been exposed to the radioactive experiments that were being carried out there. Unbeknownst to him, the spider fell from his hand and also bit a girl, Cindy Moon, on the ankle before she died from the radiation. This caused Parker to gradually acquire superhuman powers: speed, strength and agility proportional to those of a spider of his size; a "spider sense" that warns him of danger and the ability to adhere to and move along any wall or wall, regardless of its horizontality. He then manages to build a mechanism that allows him to launch a viscous chemical product in the form of a spider web.

Once prepared, he appeared on television and was an immediate success. After finishing the program, Peter let a thief escape, even though he could have easily stopped him. A few days later, when he returned home, he found that his uncle Ben had been killed by a thief, who had taken refuge in an abandoned warehouse, where the police had surrounded him. Heartbroken, he again adopted the identity of Spider-Man and quickly captured him, discovering that it was the same thief he had let escape from the television studios. From that day on, he decided to fight crime under the teaching that "With great power comes great responsibility."

Spider-Man Noir

Spider-Man Noir is an alternate version of Peter Parker in the 1930s.Driven by the idealism and values taught to him by his aunt and uncle May and Ben, reckless young Peter Parker embraces journalism as a force to fight evil and empower the innocent and needy.

After Peter mistakenly receives a clandestine report for Urich from an informant called the Fly, the young man ventures into a warehouse where the Goblin's men are unloading a shipment of stolen antiquities. An ancient but peculiar spider statue breaks and releases a horde of arachnids. One of the spiders bites Peter, causing him to lose consciousness and while unconscious, he dreams of a spider god. When he awakens, he finds himself hanging from a black web that envelops his entire body. This incident imbues Peter with superhuman abilities similar to those of a spider.

Spider-Man (Miles Morales)

Miles is the son of an African-American father and a Puerto Rican mother. He was born and raised in Brooklyn and was gifted with an aptitude for science just like his predecessor, Peter Parker.

Miles, after being awarded the last spot in the lottery for a scholarship to the charter school, visited his uncle Aaron, against the wishes of his parents, who thought Aaron was a bad influence on him.

While Miles and his uncle were discussing the opportunities the school could open for his future, the genetically enhanced spider using the OZ Formula that Aaron had stolen from Oscorp burst out of its pouch and bit Miles on the hand, causing him to lose consciousness and convulse. Later, when Miles awakens, Aaron is confronted by Jefferson Davis, Miles' father. During this brief discussion, Miles flees Aaron's apartment. While hiding from his father, Miles discovered that he had gained superhuman abilities such as camouflage, enhanced agility, and a stun ray.

Spider-Man 2009

Miguel O'Hara was shown to have an above-average IQ, so, despite his wife's opposition, George decided to tell Stone so that he would agree to enroll him in a school for gifted children. Stone, knowing that Miguel was his son, agreed and the boy entered the school run by Angela Daskalakis. After finishing school, Miguel became an expert in genetic engineering while Xina applied herself to the field of artificial intelligence.

Miguel rose rapidly within Alchemax, the corporation for which he worked, reaching the rank of head of a research department on genetic transmutations applied to man.

In a project to create genetically enhanced humans, inspired by the abilities of the 21st century Spider-Man, he adopts the secret identity of Spider-Man after accidentally gaining superhuman powers.

Spider-Gwen

Gwendolyne Maxine "Gwen" Stacy was born the daughter of George Stacy, a captain in the New York City Police Department. From an early age, she showed a fascination for music. Gwen eventually grew up to be a young student at Midtown High School where she was part of a band called The Mary Janes with her high school friends.

She also met a shy and somewhat misfit 16-year-old boy named Peter Parker, who was being bullied by his classmates. Annoyed by this abuse, Gwen defended Peter whenever she had the chance.

Later, Gwen was bitten by a spider that had been exposed to radioactive experiments. This caused Stacy to gradually acquire superhuman powers: speed, strength and agility proportional to those of a spider of her size; a "spider sense" that warned her of dangers and the ability to adhere to and move along any wall or wall, regardless of its horizontality.

Spider-Ham

Peter the spider lived in the basement of May Porker, a pig scientist seeking to create a hair spray containing nuclear particles. When May sprays herself with radiation, causing her to revert to her primal instincts, she bites Peter when he tries to help her. The release of radiation into May causes Peter to slowly lose two of his arms on his side, grow into an anthropomorphic pig much like May Porker herself. Fleeing the farm in disorientation, Peter soon realized that he still retains the abilities of a spider.

Following this surprising series of events, Peter (who adopted the surname "Porker", after his benefactor May Porker, who after the accident became mentally attuned to a state where she believed herself to be Peter's loving aunt) dedicated himself and his new found abilities to fighting injustice. May Porker's scientific genius was transferred to Peter, giving him the knowledge to devise his Web-Shooting Gauntlets to replace his lost natural ability.

Spider-Man (Ben Reilly)

Ben Reilly was a clone of Peter Parker created by the Jackal. He was estranged from Parker for years, supposedly disappearing into a five-year self-imposed exile in New York, but returned just as the original was going through a rough patch, with his Aunt May convalescing. He collaborated with Parker under the name Scarlet Spider. In a twist of events, tests later revealed that Peter was the real clone.

Peter, confused and with Mary Jane pregnant, ceded the title of Spider-Man to Ben. Reilly's time as Parker's replacement, which included possession of the Carnage symbiote among many other adventures, ended when it was revealed that Peter was not the true clone, but Reilly. He was killed by the Green Goblin, the architect of the confusing relationship between Peter and Ben. His legacy as the Scarlet Spider led his former enemy, and clone, Kaine, to convert to good.

Years later, Ben was resurrected and took the identity of the Jackal to found New U Technologies with the purpose of reanimating through cloning all the people who died directly or indirectly because of Spider-Man.

Spider-Man Cosmico

Loki, Magneto and others, fed up with always losing to their superhero arch-enemies, decide to swap their enemies in order to eliminate them forever. That and more happens in a saga from the mid-90s that affected even their own marriage.

Peter Parker is filled with energy that gives him the powers of Captain Universe, but because of an experiment by a certain Braker (a scientist who was looking for energies from other dimensions), he doesn't get all of it and has memory lapses, he doesn't remember what that energy was designated for. He has the power to fly, he shoots energy beams from both his hands and his eyes, he can modify the structure of his web to make it much more dangerous and his physical blows can destroy solar systems and even galaxies.

Meanwhile, villains he's never fought before come after him: Graviton, giant robots, sentinels... they provoke Spider-Man, but as it turns out he has amazing powers, he defeats them all without breaking a sweat. In the end, he remembers that he has the powers of Captain Universe and that the Enigma energy appears and he grants it to a certain superhero when he has to prevent a tragedy.

Spider-Girl (May Parker)

May Parker, known as "May Day", is the daughter of Peter Parker and Mary Jane on Earth-982. As a baby, she was kidnapped by the Green Goblin's hitmen. After a terrible battle against Norman Osborn in which he died, Peter Parker lost his leg, although fortunately he also recovered May, who was rescued from the clutches of the Goblin by Kaine. From that moment on, Peter decided to fully devote himself to his family, abandoning the identity of Spider-Man and entering to work as a CSI for the Police.

Years later, after displaying superhuman agility while playing a basketball game, May discovers her father's past and decides to protect the innocent as Spider-Girl. Following the defeat of the Inheritors during the Great Hunt, May changed her hero name to Spider-Woman.

Despite having promised her parents that she would never don the Spider-Girl costume again, May soon resumed her heroic activities. At first she did so in secret, facing off against Mr. Nobody in a black suit, but in her next confrontation with Crazy Eight she was already wearing her characteristic red suit. It was precisely after her first confrontation with Crazy Eight, in which she had to flee in order not to die, that she first encountered Darkdevil. This mysterious character advised her to abandon the tights, but May persisted and in her next encounter with Crazy Eight she managed to defeat him.

Scarlet Spider (Kaine Parker)

Prior to the emergence of the Spider-Man clone known as Ben Reilly, the self-proclaimed criminal the Jackal had created another clone who would later be known as Kaine. The enigmatic Kaine was the Jackal's first temporary success at cloning Spider-Man, but the cloning process proved flawed.

Over time, Kaine degenerated, gaining scars all over his face and body. The Jackal attempted to kill Kaine, but instead, he grew stronger and eventually surpassed the original Spider-Man's strength. Kaine followed in Ben Reilly's footsteps by framing him for murder, in order to give the real Peter the life he could never have.

Then Kaine went to the Jackal's ruined laboratory in order to discover who the real Spider-Man is, when he manages to extract the data from the computer a supposed defense mechanism is activated that opens a cloning capsule from which Peter Parker emerges, at that moment the Jackal arrives, Kaine searching for the truth attacks him, only to find more confusion, at that moment Scrier appears, who had been observing Kaine's movements all this time and while he was talking to the Jackal, Kaine escaped with the disk that contained the supposed truth of Spider-Man.

Spider-Woman (Jessica Drew)

Jessica Drew was the daughter of American anthropologist Johnatan Drew and his British wife Merriam. At the age of fourteen, Jessica contracted uranium poisoning due to her father's work, but Jonathan and Wyndham saved her life with a serum derived from a spider and years of hibernation in a genetic accelerator, where she received subliminal education via special tapes.

Meriem Drew was killed shortly after by a werewolf, Gregor Russoff, and while Jessica slept, the Elder God Chthon, trapped on Mount Wundagore, attempted to escape. Jonathan, now possessed by the centuries-old ghost Magnus, joined with Wyndham, now the High Evolutionary, and his evolved animal Knights of Wundagore to defeat Chthon. However, Chthon forged a mystical bond with Jessica.

After decades in hibernation, Jessica awoke having aged only to her pre-teens, and cured of all traces of radioactivity by the High Evolutionary. She had also gained superpowers due to the combination of her radiation exposure and her father's spider serum. Jessica grew up under the care of Bova, an evolved cow, but was treated with distrust by the rest of the evolved animals as the High Evolutionary became engrossed in his work.

Spider-Man Zombie

Peter Parker, had a life similar to that of his main counterpart until shortly before the clone saga, when Magneto made a deal with the zombified Sentry to weaken homo-sapiens sapiens, however, he discovered too late that the virus also infected those of his kind.

After an ordinary day, pink clouds with purple lightning appeared over Manhattan and the Avengers arrived. Spider-Man arrived and Colonel America asked him to take Ash J. Williams to a mental hospital, after insisting that monsters (which he mistook for the Zombie Sentry) called Deadites were coming to destroy everything.

As they swing, Colonel America leaps up and bites Spider-Man, causing him to fall from a great height and drop Ash. However, thanks to his special physiology, his own immune system was able to slow down the infection, allowing Parker to pose as an infected person and threaten to devour Hawkeye if they did not give in to hand over Williams. Reluctantly, the former Mightiest Heroes agree to let the traveler go, who is quickly carried by the spider to a safe rooftop. After clarifying the delay of his infection, Parker and Williams watch in horror as both humans and former heroes are infected and/or eaten.

Silk (Cindy Moon)

Cindy Moon also known as Silk was a teenager who attended the same science exhibition that Peter Parker attended where she was bitten by the same spider that turned Peter into Spider-Man. Shortly after this she was contacted by Ezekiel Sims who warned her about The Inheritors and the special role she would play in all this and to protect her from Morlun, Ezekiel locked her in a secret vault, isolating her for years from the rest of the world.

During the 10 years that Silk was imprisoned, she was always aware of Spider-Man's identity, and became a huge fan and follower of his. When the Watcher's Eye exploded, Peter discovered Cindy's existence and whereabouts. Spider-Man quickly rushed to free Cindy from her captivity, which was noticed by Morlun and his family, giving rise to The Great Hunt.

Following the defeat of the Inheritors, Cindy focused on finding her family while working for the Fact Channel and fighting crime as Silk. After finding her family and stabilizing her life, Cindy has been part of several superhero teams such as the Agents of Atlas and has recently returned to work as a journalist for Threats and Menaces.

Superior Spider-Man (Otto Octavius)

Otto Octavius, better known as Doctor Octopus or Doc Ock, was a villain and recurring character in the Spider-Man comics. He later became Spider-Man by switching bodies with Peter Parker.

After the events of Goblin Nation, Peter regained his body and Otto's mind was erased. However, in the Spider-Verse event he appeared again after a temporal anomaly as the Superior Spider-Man, only this time he had to face a new challenge: saving the Spider-Men from the Multiverse.

Although Otto died, a fragment of his consciousness survived due to a safety protocol programmed into his AI Anna and was reactivated after 100 days of rest. He then inserted himself into the Living Brain to once again possess Peter Parker's body, but later gave up on this idea and finally managed to create a new body based on his original appearance as Doctor Octopus, using the cloning technology of New U, a company created by the Jackal.

Spider-Man Asesino

Spider-Man traveled to Berlin with Need Leeds to help her write an article on spies in the Eastern Bloc. There he encountered the mutant known as Wolverine and the two of them, albeit separately, began a search for a former communist agent.

The massacre perpetrated by Charlemagne provoked a reaction from communist agents who sent numerous operatives to Berlin ready to capture the young woman. The heroes, for their part, had two different objectives: Wolverine to help her and Spider-Man to stop her. Both engaged in a brutal battle until finally Charlemagne died due to the intervention of both heroes, thus avoiding falling into the clutches of the Soviets and suffering a death a hundred times worse.

Charlemagne's death plunged Spider-Man into a deep depression as he felt guilty, so he went to the airport but before he left Wolverine found him and convinced him to atone for his guilt over Charlemagne's death, saving his sister, Alex, a CIA agent who was being held by the Soviets in a prison in the Urals.

Spider-Man decided to leave his past behind, so he abandoned Mary Jane to join Alex and Wolverine, thus becoming agents to the highest bidder.

Punk-Rock Spider-Man (Hobart Brown)

Hobie Brown is a young man who was bitten by a radioactive spider infected with illegal waste and after this event he became the Anarchic Spider-Man to fight the corruption that plagued his world.

Over time, Spider-Man was transformed into the leader of the Spider-Army, a group of people oppressed by President Osborn's regime. Hobart used his free spirit as a "radioactive suicide machine" to enlist the support of the lower classes that the regime wanted to eradicate in the name of American "strength."

This conflict led them to a final showdown against Osborn who led his forces, the Thunderbolt Department a corrupt army powered by VENOM, to eliminate their detractors. Despite having no support from the authorities or the media, Spider-Man and his group did not give up and to end the oppression Hobie used his 15,000 volt punk rock "army of amplifiers" to disable the Thunderbolts' new sybiotic technologies, which gave them the opportunity to defeat their oppressors. Spider-Man then killed Osborn and unmasked himself before the crowd.

Steam-Punk Lady Spider

May Reilly's father kept numerous animals in his studio, one of them being a spider. One day, May opened the spider's cage so that it could comfort itself, as she believed it was upset about its captivity. May then tried to pet the spider, and as soon as her hand came close, the spider bit her. According to May, the spider taught her an important lesson: don't let anyone cage you.

After her father's death, May used spare parts from her garage to create a suit with four mechanical arms that allowed her to climb walls. She also created mechanical Web-Shooters.

During a ball that May was attending, Electro took the mayor hostage. May used this as an opportunity to debut as Lady Spider. She confronted Electro and caught him; however, the rest of the Six Men of Sinestry soon arrived. After taking some plans that the mayor had hidden in his tuxedo, the six villains were forced to retreat due to the opposition posed to them by Lady Spider. Believing that she would lose the ball, May left the scene to return there.

SP//dr (Peni Parker)

Peni Parker was an ordinary 9-year-old girl except for the fact that her father was the hero known as SP//dr, who unfortunately died on his last mission from a stroke. For this reason, Peni ended up living with her uncles Ben and May, scientists of the SP//dr project. Having similar genetics, they decided to subject her to the process to use her father's armor: being bitten by a genetically modified spider that would accept her DNA.

Five years later, Peni managed to use the suit, and faced off against a new villain named Mysterio, who was able to create a fear gas, using it against Peni as they fought. However, she used her psychic link with the spider to focus and defeat him.

Later, while at school, Daredevil invited her to fight the individuals who sabotaged her father's armor five years ago (since she met him). While swinging on buildings with Daredevil, Peni asked the hero what her father was like and he told her certain details of his story. When finished, Daredevil decided to wait for more enemies to gather more information about her father's death, and so Peni chose to go home.

Spider-UK (Brian Braddock)

William Braddock is the newest member of the Captain Britain Corps, a group of Guardians of the Omniverse. While monitoring reality with a machine created by that world's Watcher, he notices multiple ongoing havoc in the Omniverse, and finds Morlun, Brix, and Bora killing multiple Spider-Men.

In one of them, he finds Morlun choking the Spider-Man of Earth-1983 after consuming him and leaving. Spider-UK then observes Earth-999 where Spider-Cat is killed by Brix and finally Daemos devouring the Spider-Man Unlimited of Earth-7831 and the multiple animal totems that inhabited there.

At that moment another individual appears, Daemos' brother Jennix, to tell him that his hunt must continue, so both of them find out that Spider-UK is watching them, and he instantly turns off the machine. In a hurry and knowing that if the power of all the spiders is consumed by 1 or more related individuals, he will have total control of the multiverse; Spider-UK runs to warn his superiors.

Spider-Man (Armored)

The Armored Spider-Man is an alternate reality version of Spider-Man. He is what would have happened if Spider-Man had stopped the robber from killing Uncle Ben. He is a playboy, billionaire, and is engaged to Gwen Stacy. He joined several other alternate universe versions of himself as part of the Spider-Men.

He is selfish and hot-headed and never considers failure and claims that he never loses. He owns a science center, a large corporate building, and a multi-million dollar company. In this reality, J. Jonah Jameson is his godfather. Uncle Ben and Aunt May are alive and live in a large mansion in Queens.

He is one of the Spider-Men summoned by Madame Web to fight Spider-Carnage and save all of reality. He has a laser built into his armor and owns a giant Spider-Robot. He was so arrogant that he fired a gas missile at the Hobgoblin without a thought for his mechanically armed teammate. He was immobilized during a fight with the Green Goblin and was unable to continue fighting, due to his rash behavior.

Ghost Spider

Peter Parker would become Spider-Man on this earth, only this time his uncle Ben would continue with him and teach him to be a better hero. With his help he would become an ambitious and powerful superhero, famous and with a lot of money, reaching the point of founding the company Parker Technologies. In order to increase his power, he uses the resources of his company to attract Spider-Men and superheroes from other universes to absorb their superpowers. Among them is the Spider-Man of Earth-616, whom he makes believe that everything has been an accident.

After a battle with the wizard Dr. Banner and his infernal Hulk, Peter Parker is left in a coma in hell. Banner will resurrect him, giving him spiritual powers and finally turning him into Ghost Spider. In a final act of kindness, he will manage to return the superheroes to their universe of origin.

Spider-Man 1602

An orphan, Peter was raised by his Scottish uncle Benjamin and his wife, until a year after his 15th birthday, Sir Nicholas Fury arrived at his door. Fury had met Peter's parents and sought Peter into his service. He became Fury's assistant, helping him with all manner of tasks. Having to remain in England, Peter had to change his surname to Parquagh as during the reign of Queen Elizabeth, the Scots were not taken favorably well due to James VI of Scotland.

Fury then sent Peter to Carlos Xavier's school for a message to be delivered when Fury's armies arrived, to prevent bloodshed. When Nick Fury planned to betray King James he released Peter into his service, telling him that he should return to his aunt and uncle. Unfortunately however, Peter was captured and interrogated by King James, then sent to Roanoke with Robert Banner to attempt to kill Fury but not before allowing him to witness the live execution of Dr. Strange. When Fury had sacrificed himself with the rest of the crew sent to kill him, he told Peter that he would let him kill him if he wanted, but Peter simply let him. After Fury and the heroes had resolved the situation with the time portal, a gamma radiation release caused Banner to jump in to protect Peter; the radiation from the portal's anomaly turned him into the Hulk; Virginia offered to let him stay with her and her father at the colony. Peter accepted, planning to get his aunt and uncle out of England to join him. He was later bitten by a spider that had been exposed to the portal's temporal energies and was gifted as a 'Witchbreed'.

As Peter grows up, he takes on the heroic identity of "The Spider" and falls in love with Virginia Dare.

Spider-Man (Bruce Banner)

Eventually, as an adult and a genius in nuclear physics, Bruce longed to make philanthropic marks in science, but was unable to receive any funding for his projects. So lacking other options, he opted to work at a SHIELD nuclear research facility at Desert Base, New Mexico.

Years later, Bruce tests one of his latest inventions, the gamma bomb (also called the G-Bomb); an atomic weapon capable of generating large amounts of gamma radiation. During the first test of the bomb, a young man named Peter Parker, motivated by a youthful dream, entered the blast zone. As a result of this event, young Parker is transformed into the Hulk.

Banner returned to work, as he began dissecting a spider that survived the same explosion that transformed Peter. The spider bit him, altering his DNA. Because he was bitten as an adult, his body was unable to cope with the mutation, and he transformed into a spider-like humanoid creature. After being on the run for two years, Bruce is captured by S.H.I.E.L.D. and becomes Spider-Man after having his mutation under control.

Six-arm Spider-man

Peter Parker was a normal boy until an accident gave him spider-like powers, turning him into the hero known as Spider-Man. Imbued with a strong sense of responsibility, the young man combined as best he could his life as an adventurer with that of a normal boy, with his job as a photographer at the Daily Bugle, a loving aunt (May Reilly) and a girlfriend (Gwen Stacy) who loved him.

However, in a moment of weakness, Peter used his scientific knowledge to try to find a way to reverse his powers so he could lead a normal life with his girlfriend, but the experiment was a failure and instead of reversing his spider-powers, they increased and he grew two extra pairs of arms.

A battle with Doc Oc showed him that his arms could be useful. Thanks to Reed Richards, he made them invisible, too, to hide them in his private life, one that he gradually abandoned. He became loved by everyone and even saved Gwen Stacy from falling to her death. And he became a spokesman for the disadvantaged and different.

Spider-Man Cyborg

This Spiderman is not very different from the one in the 616 universe, his origin is the same, Peter was a nerdy and common teenager, until he is bitten by a radioactive spider and receives powers, suffering for the death of his uncle, he understands that with great power comes great responsibility, the only difference is that the risks he ran in his career as a Superhero were very great, to the point that a confrontation with the Sinister 6 left him very badly injured, that many parts of his body had to be replaced with robot parts, becoming a Cyborg.

Cyborg, has basically the same powers as Peter Parker from Earth 616, but because his body was reinforced with technology in most of his body, he gained impressive superhuman strength, surpassing the strength he had before becoming a Cyborg, gaining more durability against physical attacks, and his robotic arm is equipped with a laser cannon, and on his back he has a sonic amplifier, with certain self-repair capabilities, and being able to connect with external technological devices, with an incredible database, although it is unknown if he can fly.
